The Dolphin Oasis

Welcome to the Dolphin Oasis, where we have been healing hundreds of injured dolphins from around our area. We have a database of information about our current residents in a table labeled, oasis with the following fields:

name
Each one has a story to tell
species
Like "Bottlenose dolphin" or "False Killer Whale"
gender
Single letter, m or f
weight
In kilograms (actually its mass)
length
In meters as decimal value
stay
Number of days the injured animal has been with us
lastfed
They are fed in the morning when our care-givers arrive at 5:00am, and should be fed after 4 hours, but no longer than every 6 hours.

Can you tell me how many dolphins we have at our oasis? (You'll need the COUNT function) 

SELECT COUNT(name) as dolphins FROM oasis

Can you tell me how many Striped dolphins we have? Striped is a species (see this picture). 

SELECT COUNT(name) as stripies
  FROM oasis
 WHERE species="Striped dolphin"

Can use use the GROUP BY command to tell me how many males and how many females we have? Why do you think the proportion isn't equal? (Hint: Since we rehabilitate injured animals, perhaps one gender gets hurt more) 

SELECT gender,COUNT(gender)
  FROM oasis GROUP BY gender

Can you use the GROUP BY and ORDER BY commands to tell me how many of each kind of dolphin we have at The Oasis? Do you know why we might have more Bottlenoses than others? 

SELECT species,COUNT(species) as how_many
  FROM oasis GROUP BY species
  ORDER BY how_many DESC

Can you figure out the average weight and length of each species and gender we care for? 

SELECT species,gender,
       AVG(length) AS avg_length,
       AVG(weight) AS avg_weight
  FROM oasis
 GROUP BY species,gender ORDER BY species
